# Domain Knowledge Reference

## U.S. Fiscal Year
- Before 1977: July 1 to June 30 (FY1953 = Jul 1952 - Jun 1953)
- After 1977: October 1 to September 30 (FY2000 = Oct 1999 - Sep 2000)
- Transition quarter: Jul-Sep 1976 (between old and new fiscal year systems)
- "FY 1963" with old system = Jul 1962 - Jun 1963

## Key Financial Terms
- **T-bills**: Treasury Bills, short-term (4-52 weeks), sold at discount
- **T-notes**: Treasury Notes, 2-10 year maturity, pay semi-annual coupon
- **T-bonds**: Treasury Bonds, 20-30 year maturity
- **TIPS**: Treasury Inflation-Protected Securities, principal adjusts with CPI
- **STRIPS**: Separate Trading of Registered Interest and Principal, zero-coupon
- **Savings bonds**: Series EE (fixed rate) and Series I (inflation-adjusted)
- **Agency securities**: Debt issued by government-sponsored enterprises (Fannie Mae, Freddie Mac)
- **Discount rate**: The difference between face value and purchase price, annualized
- **Coupon rate**: Annual interest payment as percentage of face value
- **Gross federal debt**: Total outstanding debt (public + intragovernmental)
- **Debt held by public**: Gross debt minus intragovernmental holdings
- **Intragovernmental holdings**: Debt held by government trust funds (Social Security, Medicare)
- **Budget surplus/deficit**: Receipts minus outlays (surplus = receipts > outlays)
- **ESF**: Exchange Stabilization Fund, managed by Treasury
- **SDR**: Special Drawing Rights, IMF reserve asset
- **Seigniorage**: Profit from minting coins (face value minus production cost)

## Key Policy Changes
- 1933: Gold standard abandoned domestically (Executive Order 6102)
- 1935: Social Security Act signed
- 1944: Bretton Woods agreement (fixed exchange rates)
- 1971: Nixon shock, end of gold convertibility
- 1983: Social Security Amendments (raised retirement age, taxed benefits)
- 1986: Tax Reform Act (simplified brackets)
- 2010: Dodd-Frank Act (financial regulation post-2008)
